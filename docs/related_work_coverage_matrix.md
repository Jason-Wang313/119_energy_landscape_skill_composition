# Related Work Coverage Matrix

Date: 2026-06-26

Purpose: track whether Paper 119's novelty boundary is explicit enough for a hostile reviewer. The paper should remain about a local world/action model at the skill seam: predict handoff failure, diagnose why, choose repair/probe/abstain/transition, and update future planner edge beliefs.

| Area | What prior work covers | Gap relative to Paper 119 | Boundary sentence | Current citations |

| External Validation Boundary | The local study has broad synthetic/stress/ablation coverage and a machine-checkable external audit. | It still lacks real robot or accepted high-fidelity simulation evidence, released checkpoints/logs/videos, and manifest-declared independent baseline evidence from real external runs. | Submission readiness requires the external-evidence audit to pass; until then the claim remains bounded. | Independent validation protocol; external evidence audit |

Reviewer-facing novelty boundary: Paper 119 is not primarily a contact-rich manipulation paper, a diffusion policy paper, or a general robot world-model paper. Its core claim is that skill composition needs a local world/action model at the seam: a model that predicts whether a proposed handoff will fail, diagnoses the likely reason, chooses repair/probe/abstain/transition, and stores the result for better future planning.
