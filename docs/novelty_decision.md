# Novelty Decision

Decision: STRONG_REVISE

The v5 benchmark creates a defensible novelty boundary around a local skill-seam action model: before chaining two available skills, predict whether the handoff will fail, diagnose the likely basin/barrier/descent/contact reason, choose accept/repair/probe/abstain/transition, and update planner-edge beliefs for future plans. The decisive point is not a broad claim of new energy-based robot learning; the energy terms are the implementation vocabulary for a bounded world/action-modeling interface, supported by basin-overlap posterior checks, barrier/descent seam tests, contact/dynamics guards, calibrated seam-risk screening, repair, fixed-risk abstention, and evidence that v5 beats the prior proposed composer under hard seam stress.

The idea remains risky for ICLR main without external validation, but it is now a serious local submission candidate rather than an archive scaffold.
